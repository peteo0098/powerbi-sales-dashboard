# Superstore Sales Performance Dashboard

[![View on GitHub](https://img.shields.io/badge/View_on_GitHub-100000?style=for-the-badge&logo=github&logoColor=white)](https://github.com/peteo0098/powerbi-sales-dashboard)

Project Overview
In this example, Business Intelligence Dashboards demonstrate live visibility into Sales Output, identify areas for Revenue Growth, and provide Project Performance Indicators for profitability by Business Unit. The analysis uses the Standard Superstore Sales data set, allowing you to create a Detailed Power BI report that contains both High-Level Executive Summary reports and Detailed Region and Category Analysis reports.

![Dashboard Preview](https://github.com/peteo0098/powerbi-sales-dashboard/blob/main/sales-performance-dashboard.png)

Responses to Business Questions
The primary reason for creating the dashboard was to allow fast access to key business questions. The following is a list of the key business questions answered by the dashboard: 

What is the total amount of revenue, profit, and number of orders that have been generated? 
What are the best performing product categories and sub categories?
How does the revenue trend over time and is there any noticeable seasonality?
Which geographic area's and states contribute the most profit and volume?
Who are the best performing customers?

Total Revenue: 
The company generated $2.30M in total revenue and $286,400 in total profit over those 5,000 orders through 2014-2017. 
2. Category Leaders: Between 2014-2017, the top overall revenue generator for this organization was Technology ($840,000) with Furniture as the second highest category ($740,000). 
Additionally, the highest revenue-generating subcategories of these two categories were Phones(in Technology) and Chairs(in Furniture). The West Region ($725K) is the company's top-selling region, followed closely by the East Region ($679K). 
The two dominant sales-producing states are California ($458K) and New York ($311K), which both exceed the sales of any other state.
Sales Trends: There is a clear seasonal trend, with massive revenue spikes occurring consistently in the fourth quarter (Q4) of every yea
